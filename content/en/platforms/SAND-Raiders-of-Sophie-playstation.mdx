<Section icon={Target} title="Getting Started: Your First Mission on Sophie">

If you are new to the extraction genre, the learning curve can be steep. Unlike traditional shooters, death in SAND can result in the loss of your gathered loot and, in extreme cases, your entire Trampler if you haven't saved your blueprints. The goal is to enter the desert, scavenge ruined cities and shipwrecks, and reach an extraction point before you are overwhelmed by rival crews or the encroaching sandstorms.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initialize the Trampler Editor">
    Before deploying, use the editor to construct your walking base. You must balance power (Reactors), movement (Engines), and utility (Storage). Always save your design as a **Blueprint** so you can rebuild it if your physical unit is destroyed.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Choose Your Expedition Mode">
    Select **Voyage Mode** if you want a lower-stakes experience to learn the map and gather basic resources. For high-tier loot and intense PvP, enter **Storm Dive**, where a shrinking sandstorm forces players into a central conflict zone.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Scavenge and Loot">
    Navigate to Points of Interest (POIs) such as abandoned Austro-Hungarian cities. Send crew members out on foot to enter buildings and retrieve artifacts, weapons, and ammo, while the Trampler provides mobile cover and heavy fire support.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Successful Extraction">
    Once your storage compartments are full, locate the nearest extraction zone. You must defend your Trampler during the extraction countdown. If successful, you return to the orbital station with all your gear intact.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Solo vs. Crew">
While solo play is supported, the Trampler is much easier to manage with a crew of 2-6 players. On PlayStation, utilize the built-in party chat to coordinate between the pilot, the gunner, and the ground scavengers.
</Callout>

</Section>
