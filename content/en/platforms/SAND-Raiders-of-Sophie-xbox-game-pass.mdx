<Section icon={Map} title="Game Modes: Voyage vs Storm Dive">

The core loop of the game revolves around two distinct expedition styles. Deciding which mode to play depends heavily on your crew's size, current gear level, and risk tolerance. While Voyage offers a persistent open-world environment perfect for resource gathering, Storm Dive introduces intense time pressure and high-stakes combat.

Matchmaking in both modes utilizes crew-size brackets to ensure fair engagements. Solos, small teams (2-3 players), and large crews (4-6 players) are placed on servers with scaled player caps to prevent solo players from being constantly overwhelmed by coordinated fortresses.

| Feature | Voyage Mode | Storm Dive Mode |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Risk Profile | Lower | Higher |
| Primary Objective | Resource scavenging, route learning | High-tier loot, fast-paced extraction |
| Time Limit | No fixed expedition timer | Restricted by encroaching sandstorms |
| Enemy Density | Balanced PvE and occasional PvP | High-density PvP encounters near the center |
| Matchmaking | Brackets based on team size | Brackets based on team size |

<Callout type="tip" title="Matchmaking Strategy">

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Map Exploration, Looting, and Extraction Strategies">

The procedurally generated world of Sophie is filled with diverse points of interest (POIs), including ruined cities, abandoned monuments, and massive metal shipwrecks. Exploring these areas requires careful planning. Moving too far from your Trampler leaves your main base vulnerable to theft, while staying too close limits your access to high-value loot.

When planning an expedition route, always identify multiple potential extraction zones. In Storm Dive, the encroaching sandstorm will gradually block off outer extraction points, forcing all remaining squads toward the center of the map. This creates natural choke points where high-tier PvP engagements are almost guaranteed to occur.
