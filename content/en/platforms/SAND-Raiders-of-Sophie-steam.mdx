<Section icon={Map} title="Game Modes: Voyage and Storm Dive Comparison">

The gameplay loop on Sophie is divided into two distinct expedition styles. Understanding the difference between Voyage and Storm Dive is essential for managing risk and reward. Voyage is designed for persistence and learning, while Storm Dive is a high-pressure environment where the environment is as deadly as the players.

| Feature | Voyage Mode | Storm Dive Mode |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Risk Level** | Lower | Higher |
| **Loot Profile** | Standard Scavenging | High-Tier Artifacts |
| **Time Limit** | Persistent / No Timer | Limited by Sandstorm |
| **Extraction** | Player-initiated | Center-map Focus |
| **Best For** | New Players / Testing Builds | Veterans / PvP Focused |

In Storm Dive, a sandstorm slowly engulfs the region, forcing all active Tramplers toward the center of the map. This creates a natural "bottleneck" where PvP encounters become inevitable. Voyage, conversely, allows for a more relaxed pace where crews can focus on specific resource gathering or contract completion without the immediate pressure of a closing storm.

<Callout type="warning" title="Matchmaking Brackets">
Matchmaking is based on crew size to ensure fairness. Solos will generally encounter other solos, while large crews of 4-6 players are placed in high-capacity servers.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Extraction Loop: Survival Walkthrough">

Success in SAND is defined by what you bring back to the orbital station. The extraction process is the most dangerous part of any run, as it signals your location to nearby crews who may be looking for an easy steal.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Preparation">
    Ensure your storage compartments are organized and your Trampler is fully repaired before heading toward an extraction point.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Navigation">
    Avoid open dunes where you are easily spotted. Use the ruins of cities and shipwrecks as visual cover while moving.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Scanning">
    Check the horizon for smoke or distant movement. In Storm Dive, keep a close watch on the sandstorm's proximity.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Extraction">
    Initiate the extraction sequence at a designated point. Be prepared to defend your Trampler against PvE threats and opportunistic players for the duration of the timer.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Greed Management">
The smartest raiders know when to leave. If your Trampler is heavily damaged or your storage is full of valuable resources, do not take "one last detour" to a shipwreck. Extract immediately.
</Callout>
