<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Co-op Setup Guide">

Setting up a multiplayer session in Steam Early Access is straightforward, but ensuring a stable connection and matching game modes with your friends requires a few specific steps. Before deploying, make sure all squad members have updated their game to the latest patch version to prevent version mismatch errors.

The game features two distinct deployment modes: Voyage Mode and Storm Dive. Voyage Mode offers a lower-risk environment ideal for learning the ropes, testing new Trampler builds, and gathering basic resources. Storm Dive raises the stakes significantly, introducing encroaching sandstorms that limit visibility, increase the risk of running into rival players, and offer much higher-tier rewards.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Form Your Steam Lobby">
    Launch the game and navigate to the main menu. Open your Steam Friends List overlay (Shift+Tab by default) and invite your teammates to your lobby, or use the in-game social menu to send direct squad invites.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Select Your Game Mode">
    As the lobby leader, select either Voyage Mode or Storm Dive depending on your squad's current gear level and risk tolerance. Ensure everyone is prepared for the selected hazards.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Configure Your Trampler Blueprint">
    Access the Trampler Editor before deploying. Here, your squad can build a walking fortress from scratch or load a saved blueprint. Ensure your design includes essential compartments like engines, reactors, storage, and spawn points.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Deploy to Sophie">
    Once all players have readied up, initiate the deployment sequence. Your squad will descend from the orbital station directly onto the procedurally generated desert dunes.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Mode | Risk Level | Loot Quality | Sandstorm Hazard | Best For |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Voyage Mode | Low | Common to Rare | Minimal | Solo players, testing new Trampler builds |
| Storm Dive | High | Rare to Legendary | Severe | Co-op squads, high-tier progression |

Regardless of the mode you choose, coordination inside the Trampler Editor is vital. Saving your designs as blueprints is highly recommended, as it allows you to quickly reconstruct your favorite walking fortresses if they are destroyed during a raid.

<Callout type="success" title="Extraction Tip">

<Section icon={Trophy} title="PC System Requirements & Performance Optimization">

Since matchmaking and cooperative play are currently centered on the PC platform, running the game smoothly is essential for maintaining a competitive edge during fast-paced PvPvE encounters. Framerate drops or stuttering can lead to missed shots or failed extractions.

The game utilizes BattlEye Kernel-Level Anti-Cheat to ensure a fair playing field for all raiders. This software requires manual removal if you choose to uninstall the game in the future. To help you determine if your system can handle the demanding physics and rendering of the customizable walking fortresses, consult the official system specifications.

| Hardware Component | Minimum Specification | Recommended Specification |
| --- | --- | --- |
| OS | Windows 10 64-bit | Windows 11 64-bit |
| Processor | Intel Core i5 (8th Gen) / AMD Ryzen 5 (2000 Series) | Intel Core i5 (13th Gen) / AMD Ryzen 5 (7000 Series) |
| Memory | 16 GB RAM | 32 GB RAM |
| Graphics | NVIDIA GTX 1650 / AMD RX 570 / Intel Arc A380 | NVIDIA RTX 3080 / AMD RX 6800 XT |
| DirectX | Version 11 | Version 11 |

For players running closer to the minimum specifications, lowering shadow quality, reducing view distance, and optimizing texture streaming can significantly improve framerates during chaotic battles involving multiple Tramplers.

<Callout type="info" title="Network Stability">
