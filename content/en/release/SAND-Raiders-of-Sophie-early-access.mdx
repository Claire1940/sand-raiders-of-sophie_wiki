<Section icon={Target} title="Mastering the Core Extraction Loop">

The heart of **SAND Raiders of Sophie early access** is its high-stakes extraction loop. Players enter the surface of Sophie, scavenge for resources, and must reach an extraction point to return to the orbital station. If you die or your Trampler is destroyed without a blueprint backup, you lose the resources gathered during that expedition.

The game offers two distinct ways to experience the planet Sophie, catering to different risk appetites and playstyles.

| Feature | Voyage Mode | Storm Dive Mode |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Risk Level** | Low to Medium | High |
| **Loot Quality** | Standard | High-Tier / Rare |
| **Match Style** | Persistent Open-World | Battle Royale / Shrinking Storm |
| **Timer** | No fixed limit | Limited by Sandstorm |
| **Best For** | Beginners & Resource Farming | PvP Veterans & High-Value Loot |

Matchmaking is handled via "Crew Brackets," ensuring that solo players aren't constantly overwhelmed by massive teams. The server caps are dynamically adjusted based on the size of the crews entering the region.

| Crew Size | Player Cap | Solo/Team Dynamic |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Solo** | 15 Players | Every man for himself, focused on stealth. |
| **Small Team (2-3)** | 36 Players | Balanced combat and Trampler management. |
| **Large Team (4-6)** | 45 Players | Large-scale fortress battles and boarding. |

<Callout type="warning" title="Loot Greed">
Loot greed is the leading cause of death on Sophie. In Storm Dive mode, the sandstorm moves toward the center, forcing players into tight quarters. Always prioritize extracting with a "good enough" haul rather than dying for one extra artifact.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Trampler Building and Blueprint Guide">

Your Trampler is more than a vehicle; it is your life support system. In the **SAND Raiders of Sophie early access**, the Trampler Editor allows for deep customization. You must balance engine power, reactor efficiency, and storage capacity while ensuring your crew has enough space to operate effectively.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Access the Trampler Editor">
    Before deploying, use the editor to build your walker from scratch. You can place modules like engines, reactors, and storage compartments.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Optimize Internal Layout">
    Place your spawn points and storage in hard-to-reach areas. Chaotic module placement can disorient enemies who attempt to board your vessel.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Equip Defensive Armaments">
    Bolt on mounted turrets, 80mm cannons, or autocannons. Ensure your reactor can handle the power draw of multiple weapon systems.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Save Your Blueprint">
    Crucially, save your design as a blueprint. If your Trampler is destroyed on the surface, you can rebuild it from the blueprint if you have the resources.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Essential Trampler Components:**
- **Engines:** Determine your movement speed and climbing ability.
- **Reactors:** Provide power to all active modules and weapons.
- **Storage:** Protects your scavenged loot from being lost during the run.
- **Crew Rooms:** Required to support larger teams (up to 6 players).

<Callout type="tip" title="Solo Build Strategy">
For solo players, a "Stealth Build" is often superior to a "Heavy Fortress." Use a compact layout to minimize your visual profile and focus on high-speed engines to outrun larger crews who can't match your maneuverability.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Map Locations and Loot Routes">

The planet Sophie is procedurally generated but features several recurring Points of Interest (POIs) that offer different rewards and risks. Understanding where to go—and when to leave—is the difference between a successful raider and a desert ghost.

**Key Locations to Explore:**
- **Ruined Cities:** Dense urban areas with high infantry combat and abundant weapon crates.
- **Shipwrecks:** Large structures that often contain high-value salvage and rare resources.
- **Dreadnoughts:** The most dangerous POIs, offering the chance for "WOK Bombs" and experimental gear.
- **Monuments:** Smaller ruins that serve as good intermediate stops for resources.
