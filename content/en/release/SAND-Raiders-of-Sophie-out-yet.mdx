<Section icon={Map} title="Voyage vs. Storm Dive Game Modes Explained">

Expeditions on the planet Sophie are split into two distinct game modes, catering to different risk tolerances and playstyles. Players must choose their mode carefully before launching their Trampler from the orbital station.

**Voyage Mode** serves as a persistent, lower-stakes open-world experience. There is no encroaching sandstorm timer, allowing crews to scout ruins, gather basic resources, and test Trampler configurations in a relatively controlled environment. It is the ideal starting point for solo players and new crews looking to establish their economy.

**Storm Dive Mode** is an action-heavy, high-risk battle-royale-style extraction mode. A massive sandstorm slowly engulfs the map, forcing players toward the center. This mode features high-tier loot, rare weapons, and intense PvP encounters as crews fight over the remaining extraction zones.

| Feature | Voyage Mode | Storm Dive Mode |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Risk Level | Lower | Higher |
| Loot Quality | Standard to Rare | High-Tier & Artifacts |
| Time Constraint | No fixed expedition timer | Limited by encroaching sandstorm |
| Matchmaking | Based on crew size brackets | Based on crew size brackets |
| Best Suited For | Resource farming, build testing | Coordinated PvP, high-value extraction |

Matchmaking servers are scaled dynamically based on crew sizes to ensure balanced encounters. Solos are placed in servers capped at 15 players, small teams of 2-3 are placed in servers capped at 36 players, and large crews of 4-6 face off in servers capped at 45 players.

<Callout type="tip" title="Matchmaking Strategy">
Always match your Trampler design to your crew bracket. A massive fortress build is highly inefficient for a solo player, while a small, under-armored stealth walker will quickly fall to coordinated squads in the 45-player bracket.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Starter Loop for New Raiders">

Surviving your first few hours on Sophie requires a disciplined approach to looting and base management. Follow this structured progression path to secure your first successful extraction.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Select Your Matchmaking Region">
    Log in and bind your character to your preferred region. Note that your inventory and technical progression are locked to your chosen region, though your saved Trampler blueprints can be shared across all characters.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Assemble a Basic Trampler">
    Enter the Trampler Editor at the orbital station. Install a basic engine, reactor, and at least one storage compartment. If you are playing solo, keep the layout compact so you can quickly sprint between the steering wheel and your storage chests.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Deploy into Voyage Mode">
    Launch your expedition. Steer your Trampler toward low-risk ruins and shipwrecks. Avoid the center of the map and keep an eye on the horizon for distant dust plumes, which indicate rival player movements.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Secure and Extract Your Cargo">
    Once your storage compartments are filled with scrap and components, locate an active extraction point. Defend your Trampler while the extraction sequence completes. Return to the orbital station to upgrade your gear.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Loot Category | Main Locations | Primary Use | Danger Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Scrap & Components | Abandoned ruins, street wrecks | Basic repairs, minor upgrades | Low |
| Weapons & Ammo | Shipwrecks, locked city crates | Crew defense, PvP combat | Medium |
| Rare Artifacts | Dreadnought wrecks, storm centers | High-value sales, end-game tech | High |

<Callout type="info" title="Losing Your Trampler">
If your Trampler is completely destroyed in the desert and you lack the resources to build a new one, you must either join a friend's crew or create a new character to reset your starter inventory.
</Callout>
