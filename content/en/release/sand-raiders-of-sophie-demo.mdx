<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat and Survival Strategies">

Combat in the **sand raiders of sophie demo** is chaotic and unpredictable. Battles often start as ship-to-ship artillery duels and can devolve into desperate on-foot firefights if both machines are ruined. One of the smartest design choices is how the game handles player interaction; boarding an enemy ship is difficult, requiring significant effort to bypass locked doors and limited access points.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Scavenging">
    Navigate to ruins and shipwrecks to find basic resources. Use your Trampler's height to scout for rival crews.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Ship-to-Ship Engagement">
    If a rival approaches, use long-range cannons to disable their movement or weapon systems before they get close.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="The Extraction Push">
    Once your hold is full, head to an extraction point. This is the most dangerous phase, as other players will be hunting for easy loot.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The progression system avoids common inventory management headaches by sharing loot fairly among crew members. Unwanted items can be converted into profit, which is then used to further enhance your Trampler for the next raid.

<Callout type="tip" title="Tactical Advice">
Don't always stay for the "big" loot. In the demo, many players lose everything by overstaying their welcome. Knowing when to extract is the mark of a pro raider.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Character Lore and Factions">

The characters in Sand Raiders of Sophie originate from Galicia, a region within the Austro-Hungarian Empire. This adds a unique cultural layer to the game, featuring university teachers, engineers, and former soldiers who have all ventured to Sophie to reclaim what they believe they deserve.

**Available Character Backgrounds:**
- **The Engineers**: Specialized in keeping Tramplers running under extreme pressure.
- **The Soldiers**: Former members of the Imperial army, skilled in heavy weaponry.
- **The Occultists**: Mysterious figures interested in the "ambiguous secrets" of cosmic Austria-Hungary.

The world-building emphasizes a sense of "restoring justice." Players are encouraged to treat their Tramplers as extensions of themselves, decorating them with jewelry and charms, much like a favorite horse or weapon. This personal connection to the machinery sets the game apart from more clinical sci-fi shooters.

<Callout type="success" title="Lore Fact">
