<Section icon={Swords} title="Gameplay Modes: Voyage vs Storm Dive">

Understanding the difference between the available game modes is essential before you deploy your first Trampler. SAND: Raiders of Sophie offers two distinct ways to experience the planet Sophie, catering to different risk appetites and playstyles.

| Feature | Voyage Mode | Storm Dive Mode |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Style** | Persistent Open-World | Battle-Royale Extraction |
| **Risk Level** | Lower / Moderate | Very High |
| **Loot Quality** | Standard Scavenging | High-Tier / Rare Artifacts |
| **Timer** | No fixed expedition timer | Restricted by shrinking Sandstorm |
| **Extraction** | Player-initiated at any time | Must extract before storm closure |
| **Best For** | Beginners and Resource Farming | Veteran Crews and PvP Combat |

In **Voyage Mode**, you focus on the long-term progression of your character and Trampler. It is the ideal environment for testing new builds and learning the map's procedural layout without the constant pressure of a closing circle. Conversely, **Storm Dive** is where the most valuable loot resides, but it requires a fast-moving Trampler and a crew ready for intense PvP encounters.

<Callout type="warning" title="Loot Loss Warning">
In both modes, losing your Trampler can result in significant resource loss. Always ensure you have enough materials at your base to rebuild your primary blueprints if an expedition goes south.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="How to Start Your First Expedition">

Starting your journey on Sophie requires more than just a weapon; it requires a mobile fortress. Follow these steps to ensure your first deployment doesn't end in the dunes.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Acquire and Install">
    Purchase the game via Steam or the Xbox Store. Ensure you have at least 11 GB of storage available. For PC players, make sure the **Proton BattlEye Runtime** is installed if playing on Linux or Steam Deck.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Trampler Editor">
    Before heading out, enter the Trampler Editor. Use your starting resources to place essential compartments: an **Engine** for movement, a **Reactor** for power, and **Storage** for your loot. Save this as a **Blueprint**.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Select Your Region">
    Your inventory and tech tree are tied to your specific region. Choose the server closest to you to minimize latency, as the 90-player cap can lead to intense networking demands during large-scale battles.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Deploy to Voyage">
    For your first few hours, stick to Voyage Mode. Focus on scavenging smaller ruins and abandoned streets to gather basic components and weapon ammo.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="The Extraction Loop">
    Locate an extraction point on your map. Move your Trampler to the zone and initiate the sequence. Once successful, your loot is secured in your permanent inventory, allowing you to upgrade your walking base.
  </Step>
</Steps>
