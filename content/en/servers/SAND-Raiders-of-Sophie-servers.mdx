<Section icon={Globe} title="Regional Locking and Character Progression">

One of the most important aspects of **SAND Raiders of Sophie servers** is the regional locking system. Your character's inventory, tech tree progress, and stored resources are tied specifically to the region where they were created. This means if you start your journey on the North American servers, you cannot carry your looted artifacts or upgraded weapons over to the European or Asian servers.

This structure prevents high-tier players from "server hopping" to dominate less populated regions. However, it requires players to coordinate with their friends before committing to a specific regional server. If you find yourself with no resources and no Trampler in a specific region, you may be forced to join another player's crew or start a fresh character.

<Callout type="tip" title="Blueprint Strategy">
Since blueprints are account-wide, you can design a "Budget Starter" Trampler in the editor that is available across all regions, making it easier to restart if you switch servers.

<Section icon={Users} title="Matchmaking and Crew Brackets">

The matchmaking system for **SAND Raiders of Sophie servers** is designed to prevent "unfair stomps" where a massive six-player fortress prey on a lone solo scavenger. The game actively filters sessions based on the size of your crew at the moment of deployment. This ensures that when you encounter another Trampler on the horizon, they are likely operating with a similar headcount to your own.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Form Your Crew">

  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Select Your Mode">

  <Step num="4" title="Deployment">

<Section icon={Shield} title="Anti-Cheat and Network Requirements">

To maintain the integrity of the PvPvE experience, **SAND Raiders of Sophie servers** utilize BattlEye, a kernel-level anti-cheat system. This is mandatory for all PC players. If you are playing on Linux or via a Steam Deck-style Proton setup, you must ensure the "Proton BattlEye Runtime" is installed in your Steam library, or the server will kick you upon attempted deployment.

<Section icon={Zap} title="Voyage vs Storm Dive Server Rules">

The two primary game modes offered by **SAND Raiders of Sophie servers** dictate how long a session lasts and how the world environment behaves. Voyage servers are designed for persistence and steady progression, while Storm Dive servers are governed by a shrinking sandstorm that forces player interaction.

| Feature | Voyage Mode Servers | Storm Dive Servers |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Session Timer** | Persistent (No fixed limit) | Limited (Storm-based) |
| **Loot Tier** | Standard/Reliable | High-Tier/Rare |
| **Player Density** | Spread out | Concentrated toward center |
| **Extraction** | Player-initiated anytime | Must extract before storm closure |
| **Risk Level** | Low to Medium | Very High |

In Voyage mode, the server instance remains relatively stable, allowing you to scavenge at your own pace. In contrast, Storm Dive servers act as a "Battle Royale" for extraction shooters. The server will actively close off portions of the map as the sandstorm progresses, eventually forcing all remaining crews into a final, high-stakes confrontation at the center of the map.

<Callout type="tip" title="Mode Strategy">
Use Voyage servers to farm the resources needed for high-tier Armor Plates and 80mm Cannons. Only enter Storm Dive servers once your Trampler is fully combat-ready.
</Callout>
