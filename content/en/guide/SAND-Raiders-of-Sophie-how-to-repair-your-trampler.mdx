<Section icon={Hammer} title="Structural Restoration and Blueprint Recovery">

When field repairs are no longer enough and your Trampler is completely destroyed, the game moves into the recovery phase. This is where the Blueprint system becomes your most valuable asset. The Trampler Editor allows you to save complex designs, including engine layouts, reactor placements, and weapon mounts. If your creation is turned into scrap metal by a coordinated 80 mm cannon strike, you don't have to start from scratch.

Understanding the hierarchy of repair needs is vital for efficient resource spending. In 2026, the cost of Armor Plates and high-tier reactors has been tuned upward, making "total losses" much more expensive than they were in earlier playtests.

| Component Type | Repair Priority | Consequence of Failure | Resource Cost |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Engine** | **Critical** | Zero mobility/Impossible extraction | High (Scrap + Electronics) |
| **Reactor** | **Critical** | Power loss/Weapons disabled | High (Rare Minerals) |
| **Storage** | High | Loot spill/Loss of expedition value | Medium (Metal Plates) |
| **Armor Plates** | Medium | Increased vulnerability to boarding | Low (Scrap Metal) |
| **Mounted Turrets** | Low | Reduced offensive capability | Medium (Mechanical Parts) |
