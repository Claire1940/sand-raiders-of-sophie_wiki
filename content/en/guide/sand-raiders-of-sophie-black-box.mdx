In the brutal desert world of Sophie, survival depends entirely on the capabilities of your massive walking fortress, the Trampler. The Black Key is a PvE-focused quest item used to unlock the Bismarck's secret vault, whereas the Black Box is a PvP-focused trophy used to progress your engineering capabilities.

<Section icon={Swords} title="How to Get and Farm Black Boxes">

To obtain a **sand raiders of sophie black box**, you must engage in PvP combat and fully defeat an opponent's Trampler. Simply boarding an enemy vessel and claiming their main console in the captain's quarters will allow you to steal their ship, but it will not yield a Black Box. To claim the box, you must completely destroy their machine's central reactor.

Once a Trampler's reactor is destroyed, the machine will become permanently non-functional, and the Black Box will spawn directly underneath the ruined reactor core. 

| Target Component | Strategic Effect | Priority Level | Optimal Weapon |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Reactor Core** | Instantly destroys ship, drops Black Box | ★★★★★ | 80mm Cannon |
| **Trampler Legs** | Immobilizes target, prevents escape | ★★★★☆ | 40mm Sustained Fire |
| **Mounted Turrets** | Disables enemy defense and firepower | ★★★☆☆ | Heavy Ordnance |
| **Captain's Cabin** | Allows boarding to stop player respawns | ★★★☆☆ | Small Arms / C4 |

<Callout type="warning" title="Physics Glitch Warning">
In the current Early Access build of 2026, Black Boxes have a known physics issue where they can clip through the desert floor if dropped directly on the ground. To avoid losing your hard-earned loot, pick up the box immediately upon destruction and place it directly onto your Trampler's cargo racks.
</Callout>

To farm these boxes efficiently, follow this step-by-step combat workflow:

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Equip Heavy Cannons">
    Mount a balanced combination of 40mm and 80mm cannons on your Trampler. The 40mm is perfect for sustained shield pressure, while the 80mm delivers the massive burst damage required to smash heavy armor plates.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Immobilize the Legs">
    Aim your initial shots at the moving legs of the enemy Trampler. Destroying the legs slows down their movement, preventing them from escaping behind sand dunes or reaching an extraction zone.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Target the Reactor Core">
    Once the target is stationary, focus all firepower on the reactor core located at the center of the enemy ship. Watch for the bright explosion indicating the reactor has blown.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Retrieve and Secure the Box">
    Board the smoking wreckage, locate the Black Box sitting directly under the destroyed reactor, and carry it back to your ship's racks.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Advanced PvP Combat Strategies for Hunters">

Farming Black Boxes means hunting other players, which carries immense risk. To shift the odds in your favor, you must master the art of the slow, grinding Trampler brawl. 

The absolute easiest way to secure PvP victories is to play **solo**. The matchmaking system queues solo players exclusively against other solos. Solo ships are typically smaller, faster, and carry significantly less armor and protection around their reactor cores, making them much easier targets for a quick ambush.

<Callout type="success" title="Boarding Tactics">
If trading cannon fire is proving too costly, try physically ramming your Trampler into the enemy's ship. Breach their captain's cabin and interact with their console. This stops them from respawning, allowing you to safely dismantle their reactor with C4 or time bombs to claim the Black Box!
</Callout>

Always utilize the desert dunes to force your opponents into awkward, slow firing angles. Keep a constant eye on your internal systems and repair continuously during a fight; attrition is often the deciding factor in these massive mechanical brawls.

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Safe Extraction Strategies">

Finding a Black Box is only half the battle; you must safely extract from Sophie's surface to secure your loot. Anything left unsecured on your Trampler can be lost if you are defeated or fail to reach the extraction ship before time runs out.

| Extraction Phase | Action Required | Danger Level | Pro Tip |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. Positioning** | Park Trampler on flat ground near radio tower | Low | Avoid stopping on rocks or awkward slopes |
| **2. Evacuation Call** | Climb radio tower and interact with the radio | Medium | Watch for snipers while climbing the tower |
| **3. Defense** | Defend the green smoke area from incoming players | High | Take high-ground cover and watch for boarders |
| **4. Boarding** | Grab the extraction rope once the ship arrives | Medium | Do not stay inside; you must physically climb the rope |

<Callout type="info" title="Extraction Mechanics">
Remember that your character does not escape by sitting inside the Trampler. The extraction ship will pull your Trampler away separately, but you must physically grab the dangling extraction rope yourself to successfully escape.
</Callout>

Before heading to an extraction zone, open your map and identify the safest white icon. Do not simply pick the closest option if it is surrounded by active combat markers or visible enemy Tramplers.
