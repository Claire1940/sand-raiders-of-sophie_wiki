<Section icon={Shield} title="Building Your First Trampler">

Your Trampler is your most valuable asset. In the early game, you will likely start with a basic model, but the **Trampler Editor** allows for significant customization. One of the most important **SAND Raiders of Sophie beginner tips** is to prioritize functional layouts over aesthetic ones. A confusing interior might look cool, but it can hinder your crew's ability to repair critical components during a boarding action.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Access the Editor">
    Enter the Trampler Editor from the main hub before deploying. This is a safe zone where you can experiment with module placement without risking resources.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Install Core Modules">
    Place your **Engine** and **Reactor** in protected areas. These are the heart of your machine; if they go down, you are a sitting duck in the desert.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Expand Storage">
    Add **Storage Compartments** early. Your character's inventory is small, so the Trampler acts as your primary trunk for all scavenged goods.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Save Your Blueprint">
    This is critical. Blueprints are shared across your characters. If your Trampler is destroyed, you can use a blueprint to rebuild it quickly provided you have the funds.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Blueprint Management">
Never deploy without saving a blueprint of your current configuration. Rebuilding a complex fortress from memory while your resources are low is a recipe for frustration.
</Callout>

| Module Type | Essential Function | Placement Tip |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Engine** | Movement and speed | Internal, heavily armored |
| **Reactor** | Power for weapons/systems | Central, easy access for repairs |
| **Crew Room** | Spawn point and crew capacity | Near the steering station |
| **Storage** | Loot capacity | Lower levels for easy offloading |
| **Armor Plates** | Damage reduction | External, focused on front/sides |

</Section>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat and Scavenging Tactics">

Combat in the desert is two-fold: massive Trampler-on-Trampler warfare and tight, first-person infantry skirmishes. When scavenging cities or ruins, you are often vulnerable to "Ironclads"—NPC threats—and other players who might try to board your vehicle while you are away.

**Combat Tier List for Beginners:**
- **S-Tier**: **Repair Kits**, **Storage Compartments**, **Mounted Turrets**. These are the backbone of any successful expedition.
- **A-Tier**: **80 mm Mounted Cannon**, **Shotgun Cannons**, **Armor Plates**. Essential for defending your base from aggressive rivals.
- **B-Tier**: **Autocannons**, **Smoke Grenades**, **M1866/9 Einzel**. Reliable tools for mid-range engagements and escapes.
- **C-Tier**: **WOK Bombs**, **Rare Experimental Weapons**. Powerful but situational and difficult to obtain early on.

<Callout type="success" title="Crew Coordination">
If playing in a crew, assign roles. One person should always stay near the Trampler to handle repairs and man the turrets, while others scavenge. A lone Trampler is an easy target for boarders.
</Callout>

| Location Type | Common Loot | Threat Level | Scavenging Tip |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Abandoned Cities** | Ammo, Basic Guns, Fuel | Medium | Stay in the shadows; watch for snipers. |
| **Desert Ruins** | Scrap Metal, Tech Parts | Low | Good for quick resource runs. |
| **Sunken Ships** | Rare Components, Engines | High | Bring a crew; these are often camped. |
| **Dreadnoughts** | End-game Gear, WOK Bombs | Extreme | Only approach with a fully armored fortress. |

</Section>
