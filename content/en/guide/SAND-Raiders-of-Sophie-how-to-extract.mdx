<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Guide: How to Extract Successfully">

The actual process of **SAND Raiders of Sophie how to extract** involves a series of steps that begin the moment you decide your cargo hold is full enough to justify the risk of a return trip.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Locate an Extraction Zone">
    Open your map and identify the nearest active extraction point. In Storm Dive, these points may disappear as the sandstorm closes in, so always have a secondary route planned.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Clear the Immediate Area">
    Before initiating the extraction, scout the perimeter. Rival players often "camp" extraction zones. Use long-range optics or send a crew member on foot to ensure no hostile Tramplers are lying in wait.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Initiate the Sequence">
    Position your Trampler within the extraction radius. You will typically see a timer or a progress bar. During this time, your Trampler is a stationary target, making defensive positioning vital.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Defend the Fortress">
    Expect PvE "undead" or rival raiders to attack once the extraction flare or signal is active. Use your mounted Shotgun Cannons for close-range defense and Autocannons for sustained pressure.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Secure Departure">
    Once the timer hits zero, the extraction is complete. You will be returned to the orbital station with your loot, resources, and Trampler intact. Blueprints for any destroyed parts can be used to rebuild for the next run.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Managing Risks and Contested Zones">

Extraction is the most dangerous phase of any expedition. Rival crews know that this is when you are at your most vulnerable and your cargo hold is at its heaviest. To survive, you must recognize danger signals before they turn into a total loss of progress.

Common danger signals include smoke on the horizon (indicating another Trampler), the sound of distant cannon fire, or the visual shimmer of the sandstorm in Storm Dive mode. If you see multiple Tramplers converging on a single extraction point, it may be wiser to rotate to a more distant, less populated zone.
