The highly anticipated release of SAND has players asking about the **sand raiders of sophie developers** and their vision for this unique dieselpunk extraction shooter. Developed as a collaborative project, the game combines the expertise of two distinct European game development studios under the publishing umbrella of tinyBuild. This partnership has allowed the teams to create an ambitious PvPvE sandbox that merges vehicular combat with tactical extraction mechanics.

### Hologryph: The Ukrainian Powerhouse
Based in Lviv, Ukraine, Hologryph is the lead studio spearheading the development of the game. Founded in 2016 by a small group of passionate gaming enthusiasts, Hologryph has grown into a highly respected developer. They are best known for creating *Secret Neighbor*, the massively popular multiplayer spin-off in the *Hello Neighbor* franchise, as well as co-developing *Party Hard 2*. In November 2020, tinyBuild acquired a majority stake in Hologryph, securing a long-term partnership that paved the way for the development of their next-generation intellectual property: *SAND: Raiders of Sophie*.

### TowerHaus: Collaborative Co-Developers
Partnering with Hologryph is TowerHaus, a studio that brings deep technical expertise to the table. TowerHaus has co-developed the game's intricate mechanics, focusing on the massive scale of the game's open world and its complex physics-based vehicular movement. Together with Hologryph, they have spent years designing the modular systems that govern the "Tramplers"—the giant walking mech fortresses that serve as the players' primary home, transport, and weapon on the desert planet of Sophie.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Core Gameplay Loop and Modes">

In the brutal alternate-1910 setting of Sophie, players must master the mechanics of the modular walking mechs known as Tramplers. Survival on the dunes depends on planning, coordination, and rapid adaptation. Players can choose between two distinct gameplay modes depending on their preferred style of play: Voyage Mode and Storm Dive.

| Feature | Voyage Mode | Storm Dive |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Time Limit | No time limit | Shrinking sandstorm circle |
| Extraction Availability | Available from the start of the run | Appears only partway through |
| Risk Level | Moderate (good for beginners) | High (increased player encounters) |
| Loot Quality | Standard tier items | High-tier rewards and artifacts |

To help new players navigate their first drop, the development team recommends a structured approach to preparing their Trampler and executing a successful extraction run.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Select a Blueprint">
    Before deploying, choose a Trampler blueprint in the editor. Beginners should select the Grumpy Walker preset, as it is cheap to replace and offers well-rounded starting stats.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Load Your Armaments">
    Nothing spawns loaded. Your first task upon dropping is to transfer complete ammo stacks from your storage racks directly into your cannons and sidearms using the Tab key.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Scavenge the Dunes">
    Pilot your Trampler to cities, shipwrecks, and ruins. Turn off your engine when parked to prevent emitting black smoke, which alerts rival players to your exact location.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Extract with Loot">
    Secure your loot in green boxes on your Trampler racks. Navigate to an active extraction point, climb down from your mech, and use the extraction rope to escape to the orbital station.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Scavenger Tip">
Always use binoculars to scan the horizon. A distant column of black smoke indicates an active enemy Trampler. Turning off your own engine when stopped keeps you hidden and saves valuable fuel.
</Callout>

</Section>
