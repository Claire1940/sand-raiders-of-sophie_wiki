To successfully maneuver a Trampler, pilots must coordinate several core systems simultaneously. Driving is handled from the bridge, but physical compartments throughout the machine dictate your speed, power distribution, and structural integrity.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Fuel the Reactor Core">
    Locate the reactor compartment inside your Trampler. Load solid fuel blocks or fuel cells into the core to initialize power. Running out of fuel during an expedition leaves your machine completely immobilized and highly vulnerable to rival crews.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Access the Piloting Station">
    Head to the bridge and interact with the steering console. This console gives you direct control over the walking legs. You can steer using standard directional controls while monitoring your heading on the compass.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Manage Engine Heat and Speed">
    Toggle between movement speeds. High-speed sprints drain fuel rapidly and generate significant engine heat. Monitor the temperature gauges to prevent temporary system lockouts or engine damage.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Utilize Auxiliary Systems">
    Deploy smoke grenades for visual cover or use physical armor plates to absorb incoming cannon fire. Ensure your storage compartments are locked down before engaging in high-speed maneuvers.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Engine Overheating">
Sprinting for prolonged periods will overheat the reactor. If the temperature gauge fills completely, the legs will lock, leaving you stranded in the open desert until the system cools down.
</Callout>
