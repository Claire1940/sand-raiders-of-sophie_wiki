<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initialize Your Trampler">
    Before deploying, use the Trampler Editor to configure your base. For beginners, focus on a balanced layout with at least one storage compartment and basic armor plating. Save this as a blueprint so you can quickly rebuild if your vehicle is destroyed.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Select Your Mode">
    Choose **Voyage Mode** for your first several runs. This mode provides a persistent open-world experience with lower stakes, allowing you to learn the map layout and resource locations without the crushing pressure of a closing sandstorm.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Scavenge and Loot">
    Target abandoned cities and shipwrecks. Look for weapon crates and resource piles. Your Trampler acts as your primary storage, so frequently return to your vehicle to offload items from your personal inventory.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Defend and Extract">
    Keep an eye on the horizon for smoke or mechanical movement. If you encounter other players, decide whether to fight or flee based on your current loot value. Reach an extraction point and defend it until the orbital transport arrives.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Trampler Builds and Blueprint Management">

Your Trampler is more than a vehicle; it is a mobile fortress, storage unit, and spawn point. The modular building system allows for extreme customization, but successful players generally stick to specific "roles" depending on their crew size and objectives.

  <Card title="The Stealth Scout">

  </Card>
  <Card title="The Heavy Fortress">
    - **Maximum Armor**: Stacked plates to survive cannon fire.

    - **Crew Required**: Needs 4-6 players to manage all systems.
  </Card>
  <Card title="The Cargo Hauler">
    - **Expanded Storage**: Multiple compartments for high-volume loot.
    - **Defensive Maze**: Chaotic interior layouts to deter boarders.
    - **Voyage Specialist**: Best for long-duration resource farming.
  </Card>

Effective building requires balancing power consumption with weight and utility. Use the table below to prioritize your module upgrades.

| Module Type | Priority | Primary Function | Key Requirement |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Engines** | High | Determines movement speed and hill-climbing ability | Sufficient Fuel/Power |
| **Reactors** | Essential | Powers all electronics, turrets, and doors | Internal Space |
| **Storage** | Essential | Protects loot from being lost on death | Central Placement |
| **Spawn Points** | High | Allows crew members to respawn inside the Trampler | Crew Room Access |
| **Armor Plates** | Medium | Absorbs damage from cannons and explosions | Weight Capacity |

<Callout type="tip" title="Building Strategy">
Place your most valuable compartments (Storage and Reactor) in the center of the Trampler. Use "dummy" compartments or empty rooms on the exterior to act as a buffer against boarding attempts and explosive damage.
</Callout>
