<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Refueling Process">

The actual act of refueling involves a specific set of actions that leave the player vulnerable. In **SAND: Raiders of Sophie**, you cannot refuel from the pilot's seat. You must physically move to the reactor room and perform the maintenance.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Locate Fuel in Inventory">
    Ensure you have scavenged fuel canisters or rods in your personal inventory or the Trampler's storage compartment.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Access the Reactor Module">
    Walk to the internal compartment where your **Reactor** is installed. Look for the interaction prompt labeled **Open Fuel Port**.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Insert the Fuel Source">
    Drag the fuel item from your inventory into the reactor slot. You will see the **Energy Level** bar begin to rise immediately.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Monitor Heat Levels">
    High-grade fuel can sometimes cause reactor overheating. Check the temperature gauge and apply **Coolant** if the bar enters the red zone.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Secure the Compartment">
    Close the reactor interface and return to the bridge. Confirm the updated **Estimated Range** on your HUD before resuming travel.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Optimizing Engine and Reactor Efficiency">

Refueling becomes less of a chore if your Trampler is built for efficiency. The **Trampler Editor** allows you to customize every aspect of your machine, including how it consumes power. Heavier armor and more mounted turrets increase the strain on your engines, leading to faster fuel consumption.

When designing your build, consider the balance between power generation and power draw. A massive fortress with only one basic reactor will require constant refueling, making it impractical for long-distance expeditions in **Voyage Mode**.

| Optimization Strategy | Benefit | Drawback |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Dual Reactor Setup** | Doubles energy capacity and output | Increased weight and takes up more module space |
| **Lightweight Legs** | Reduces fuel consumption during movement | Lower durability and lower carry capacity |
| **Power Shunting** | Directs energy only to active systems | Requires manual management during high-stress combat |
| **Upgraded Injectors** | Increases energy gained per fuel rod | Requires rare resources to craft or buy |
