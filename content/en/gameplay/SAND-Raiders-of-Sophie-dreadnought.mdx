<Section icon={Target} title="Understanding the SAND Raiders of Sophie Dreadnought">

The **SAND Raiders of Sophie dreadnought** represents the pinnacle of the extraction experience on the fallen planet Sophie. Unlike standard ruins or abandoned cities, the Dreadnought is a massive, stationary mechanical remnant that serves as a lightning rod for the most geared crews in the server. It is not merely a loot location; it is a tactical arena where the environment and rival players converge to test your Trampler's durability and your crew's coordination.

In the current 2026 meta, the Dreadnought has become the only reliable source for experimental technology and the devastating WOK Bomb. Because the game utilizes a procedural world structure, the exact orientation of the Dreadnought may shift, but its status as a high-tier loot hub remains constant across both Voyage and Storm Dive modes.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Trampler Builds for Dreadnought Infiltration">

Your Trampler is your lifeline when approaching the **SAND Raiders of Sophie dreadnought**. Because this zone is a magnet for PvP, a "Stealth and Escape" build is rarely viable unless you are a solo player with exceptional timing. For most crews, a "Heavy Fortress" or "Balanced Raider" build is necessary to survive the gauntlet of mounted turret fire.

The 2026 pricing for Armor Plates has been increased, making every plate a significant investment. When building for a Dreadnought run, you must balance protection with storage capacity. If you secure the loot but have no room to store it within your internal compartments, the run is a failure.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Core Compartment Layout">
    Place your Reactor and Engines deep within the internal structure. Use chaotic module placement to disorient boarders who managed to grapple onto your Trampler during the chaos.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Weaponization">
    Mount at least one 80 mm Mounted Cannon for long-range deterrence and multiple Shotgun Cannons for close-range defense against boarding parties.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Defensive Layering">
    Apply heavy Armor Plates to the front and legs of the Trampler. Since the Dreadnought is often elevated, you will likely take fire from above and below simultaneously.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Crew Room Optimization">
    Ensure you have enough Crew Rooms to support a full team. A 6-player crew can rotate between steering, gunnery, and active repairs, which is essential for surviving the Dreadnought's perimeter.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Tactics: Defending the Hull">

Combat at the **SAND Raiders of Sophie dreadnought** is split into two phases: Trampler-to-Trampler warfare and on-foot boarding actions. While your mounted cannons handle the external threats, your crew must be prepared for close-quarters combat within the Dreadnought's tight corridors.

The M1866/9 "Einzel" Breechloader is a reliable choice for these encounters, especially for those who unlocked the exclusive skin during the Server Slam event. Smoke Grenades are also invaluable for repositioning when a rival crew pins you down in a loot room.

| Combat Scenario | Preferred Weapon | Tactical Advice |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Trampler Defense** | 80 mm Cannon | Aim for the enemy's legs to immobilize them. |
| **Room Clearing** | Shotgun / Einzel | Use corners and height advantages. |
| **Escaping Boarders** | Smoke Grenades | Break line of sight and return to the pilot seat. |
| **Long-range Sniping** | Experimental Railgun | Target exposed crew members on enemy decks. |

<Callout type="info" title="Pro Tip: The Solo Rat">
If playing solo, do not bring a massive Trampler to the Dreadnought. Use a small, stealthy build, park it in a nearby ruin, and sneak in on foot. You can often scavenge the leftovers of a massive battle without being noticed.
</Callout>
