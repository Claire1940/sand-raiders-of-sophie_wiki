Building a functional walker requires careful planning of your power grid and module accessibility. Follow these steps to ensure your first build is desert-ready:

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Place Core Modules">
    Start with the engine and reactor. These determine your movement speed and how many active modules (like turrets or scanners) you can power simultaneously.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Install Storage and Spawn Points">
    Add storage compartments for your loot and spawn points so your crew can respawn inside the Trampler during an expedition.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Layer Armor and Defense">
    Place armor plates on vulnerable sections like the engine room. Install mounted weapons like Autocannons or Shotgun Cannons for close-range defense.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Plan the Interior">
    Organize crew rooms and hallways. Use a chaotic layout if you want to disorient enemies who attempt to board your vessel.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Target} title="Voyage vs. Storm Dive: Game Modes">

Understanding the difference between the two primary modes is essential for progressing in **SAND Raiders of Sophie gameplay**. Each mode caters to a different risk appetite and provides different tiers of rewards.

| Feature | Voyage Mode | Storm Dive Mode |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Risk Level** | Low to Medium | High |
| **Primary Goal** | Persistent exploration and farming | Fast-paced loot and survival |
| **Timer** | No fixed expedition timer | Limited by encroaching sandstorm |
| **Loot Quality** | Standard resources and weapons | High-tier artifacts and rare gear |
| **PvP Frequency** | Moderate (depends on server) | High (players forced to center) |
| **Matchmaking** | Crew-size based brackets | Crew-size based brackets |

<Callout type="success" title="Mode Strategy">
New players should spend their first 5-10 hours in **Voyage Mode**. This allows you to learn the map's procedural layout and master Trampler handling without the constant pressure of a shrinking storm circle.
</Callout>

Voyage Mode is perfect for testing new Trampler builds or gathering the basic materials needed for high-end upgrades. Storm Dive, conversely, is a battle-royale-style extraction experience where the last remaining crews often clash near the center of the map for the highest-value loot.

</Section>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Weapons and Combat Tier List">

Combat in **SAND Raiders of Sophie gameplay** is split between handheld infantry combat and ship-to-ship Trampler warfare. Your loadout should include a mix of reliable personal firearms and heavy mounted ordnance.

| Weapon Name | Type | Tier | Best Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |

<Callout type="warning" title="Anti-Cheat Notice">
The game uses **BattlEye** kernel-level anti-cheat. Ensure your system is compatible and that you have installed the Proton BattlEye Runtime if playing on Linux or Steam Deck to avoid being kicked from matches.
</Callout>

When engaging in Trampler combat, aim for the enemy's legs to immobilize them or the engine room to disable their power. If you are boarded, use Smoke Grenades to create confusion and rely on high-spread weapons like Shotgun Cannons to clear your decks.

Loot is generally found in three types of locations. **Ruined Cities** offer high densities of ammo and weapons but are hotspots for PvP. **Shipwrecks** often contain rare salvage and artifacts but have limited escape routes. **Monuments and Ruins** are safer for solo players but offer lower-tier resources.

<Callout type="tip" title="The Dreadnought Risk">
The Dreadnought is a special POI that offers the highest rewards, including a chance for the **WOK Bomb**. Only approach this area if your crew is fully armed and your Trampler has reinforced armor plates, as it is a magnet for the server's most aggressive players.
</Callout>
