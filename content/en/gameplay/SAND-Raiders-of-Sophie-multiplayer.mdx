<Section icon={Map} title="Multiplayer Modes: Voyage vs. Storm Dive">

In **SAND Raiders of Sophie multiplayer**, players must choose between two primary expedition styles. Each mode dictates the level of risk, the potential rewards, and the frequency of player-versus-player interactions. Choosing the right mode for your crew's current gear level is the difference between a successful haul and a total loss of resources.

| Feature | Voyage Mode | Storm Dive Mode |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Risk Level** | Low to Medium | High |
| **Pacing** | Persistent Open-World | Battle Royale / Extraction Hybrid |
| **Loot Quality** | Standard / Reliable | Rare / High-Tier |
| **Timer** | No fixed limit | Limited by encroaching Sandstorm |
| **PvP Frequency** | Moderate | Very High |
| **Best For** | Learning routes, building resources | High-stakes combat, Artifact hunting |

Voyage Mode serves as the foundation for progression. It allows crews to explore Sophie at their own pace, test new Trampler blueprints, and gather the materials needed for high-end reactors and engines. It is the ideal environment for a new squad to practice communication before attempting the more grueling Storm Dive.

<Callout type="tip" title="Strategy Note">
In Storm Dive, the sandstorm forces all active Tramplers toward the center of the map. This creates "choke points" where PvP is inevitable. Ensure your crew has at least one dedicated **Engineer** with a full stack of **Repair Kits** before entering this mode.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Crew Roles and Coordination">

Success in **SAND Raiders of Sophie multiplayer** is impossible without clear communication. A Trampler is a complex machine that requires multiple systems to be operated simultaneously during a crisis. For crews of 4-6 players, assigning specific roles ensures that the walking base remains mobile and lethal even under heavy fire.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="The Pilot (Navigation)">
    The Pilot is responsible for steering the Trampler and managing engine heat. In multiplayer, the Pilot must coordinate with the crew to position the base for optimal firing angles while avoiding environmental traps or sand-traps.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Gunners (Firepower)">
    Gunners man the mounted turrets and cannons. They must prioritize targets, such as rival player Trampler legs or incoming PvE threats. Coordination between Gunners is key to focus fire on a single module to disable an enemy base quickly.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="The Engineer (Maintenance)">
    The most overlooked but vital role. The Engineer stays inside the Trampler to manage the **Reactor**, handle **Repair Kits**, and extinguish fires. They also monitor the **Storage Compartments** to ensure loot isn't lost during boarding actions.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="The Boarders (Assault)">
    When two Tramplers get close, the Boarders use handheld weapons to leap onto the enemy base. Their goal is to kill the enemy crew and sabotage their internal modules, such as the engine or spawn points.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Critical Role Tip">
If your Engineer runs out of Repair Kits during a Storm Dive, your Trampler's survivability drops by nearly 70%. Always prioritize storage space for utility items over extra loot in high-risk zones.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Trampler Builds for Squad Play">

Building a Trampler for **SAND Raiders of Sophie multiplayer** requires a different mindset than solo play. You need to account for crew rooms, internal walkways, and overlapping fields of fire. A "mechanical furniture" build will quickly fall apart under the pressure of a coordinated squad.

  <Card title="The Stealth Raider">

    - **Best For**: 2-3 player crews focusing on city looting.
    - **Pros**: Hard to spot on the horizon; fast extraction.
    - **Cons**: Vulnerable if caught by a heavy build.
  </Card>
  <Card title="The Boarding Specialist">
    - **Focus**: Multiple Spawn Points and chaotic internal layouts.

    - **Cons**: Requires high player skill in close-quarters combat.
  </Card>

When designing your blueprint in the editor, consider the "path of least resistance" for your Engineer. If the Reactor is on the top floor and the Repair Station is in the basement, your maintenance time will be doubled. Keep essential modules clustered for efficiency.

<Callout type="success" title="Blueprint Sharing">
Remember that while inventory is tied to your character's region, **Trampler Blueprints** are shared across all your characters. You can design a masterpiece on one character and deploy it with another as long as you have the resources.
</Callout>
