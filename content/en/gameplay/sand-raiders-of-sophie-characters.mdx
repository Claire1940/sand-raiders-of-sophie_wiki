### How to Create a New Character

Creating a new character is straightforward but must be done with your target server region in mind. Because your inventory and progression do not carry over between different regions, choosing the correct server at the start is vital.

To create a new character, navigate to the **Settings** menu and click on **Change Character**. Once you are redirected to the character creation screen, click on the plus (+) icon, select your character model, customize your look, and click **Create**.

### How to Delete a Character

If you have created multiple characters and want to clean up your roster, you can delete them permanently. Be extremely cautious when doing this, as all progression, inventory items, and faction reputations tied to that specific character will be lost forever.

<Section icon={Shield} title="How Character Progression and Regional Lock Work">

In *SAND: Raiders of Sophie*, your progression is deeply tied to the specific character you play. Unlike other survival extraction shooters where your account shares a single stash, this game utilizes character-specific progression loops. This design encourages players to build unique characters for different playstyles or dedicated squad configurations.

The most critical mechanic to understand is the **Regional Server Lock**. When you create a character, that character is bound to the server region (such as North America, Europe, or Asia) you selected at the time of creation. If you switch server regions to play with friends in another part of the world, your characters from your home region will not appear. They are not deleted; they are simply inaccessible until you switch back to your original region.

### Tech Trees and Blueprints

Your character's progression is primarily measured by their progress in the Tech Tree. The Tech Tree is split across three distinct factions: Godlewski's Expedition, K.K. Landwehr, and Kaiser's Friends. Unlocking recipes in these trees requires Crowns (the game's currency) and rare materials like Ficus, Crystal, and Black Box items scavenged from the dunes.

- **Godlewski's Expedition**: Unlocks structural and propulsion upgrades, including advanced engines, chassis corridors, and crew facilities.
- **K.K. Landwehr**: Manages weaponry, armor plating, artillery decks, steering, and throwables like Time Bombs.
- **Kaiser's Friends**: Handles cargo systems, specialized cannons, and unique chassis variants.

While your inventory and tech tree progression are strictly character-bound, **Trampler Blueprints are shared account-wide**. This means any blueprint you design and save in the Trampler Editor can be accessed and built by any character on your account, regardless of their individual progression level.

<Callout type="info" title="Progression Tip">
If you lose all of your resources and cannot afford to build a new Trampler, you do not need to delete your character. You can join a friend's crew as a deckhand to earn Crowns, or create a temporary second character to gather basic resources.
</Callout>

### Preparing Your Trampler and Personal Gear

Before embarking on an expedition, you must choose a Trampler blueprint. For new characters, the **Grumpy Walker** preset is highly recommended. It is cheap to rebuild if you lose it in combat and features well-rounded stats that are perfect for learning the ropes.

On your person, keep your starter loadout simple but effective:
- **Armor**: Basic chest and head protection to mitigate bullet damage from other players.
- **Weapons**: A dual-weapon setup consisting of a revolver for close-quarters self-defense and a rifle for picking off enemies at a distance.
- **Rations**: A stack of canned food to manage your hunger and maintain stamina.
- **Fuel**: A few fuel rods to keep your Trampler powered during long journeys.

<Callout type="tip" title="Pre-Deployment Ammo Check">
Nothing spawns loaded when you drop into a match. Your personal guns and your Trampler's heavy cannons will appear empty. Your first task upon landing must be to manually transfer ammo stacks from your storage boxes and load every weapon before moving your vehicle.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="The Galician Raiders: Lore and Character Archetypes">

The characters you play in *SAND: Raiders of Sophie* are not highly trained soldiers or heroic explorers. Instead, they are desperate outcasts, wage earners, and descendants of laborers from Galicia, a poor region within the Austro-Hungarian Empire. 

In this alternate-history 1910 setting, humanity successfully colonized distant cosmic territories. The Kaiser promised the Galicians a massive portion of planet Sophie to entice them to volunteer as the primary construction force. However, an ecological disaster of mysterious origin devastated the planet, transforming its oceans into endless deserts and leaving behind abandoned imperial cities. 

Driven by determination to rectify historical injustices and claim what they believe is rightfully theirs, these raiders return to the ruins. They risk their lives against supernatural threats like the undead Upiors and other desperate scavengers.

### Character Backgrounds and Motivations

The raiders on Sophie come from various walks of life, each bringing their own unique perspective and skills to the desert:

- **Galician Wage Earners**: Desperate laborers trying to survive and claim the land promised to their ancestors.
- **University Researchers**: Half-crazy academics and occultists seeking to unravel the cosmic secrets behind the planet's sudden disaster.
- **Former Soldiers**: Veterans of the imperial military who use their combat expertise to dominate the dunes and protect their crews.
- **Dieselpunk Engineers**: Brilliant mechanics who specialize in designing, modifying, and repairing the massive walking Tramplers.

### Mastering the Extraction Loop

Extraction is the most dangerous phase of any raid. In *SAND: Raiders of Sophie*, you cannot simply log off to save your progress. You must physically guide your Trampler to an extraction tower, climb the ladders, and activate the radio. This triggers a 90-second countdown and releases bright green smoke, alerting every hostile player in the area to your location.

<Callout type="tip" title="Extraction Security">
Never turn off your Trampler's engine during extraction if you are playing in a squad. Keep one crew member inside the vehicle to man the turrets and scout for incoming hostiles while another climbs the tower to activate the radio.
</Callout>
