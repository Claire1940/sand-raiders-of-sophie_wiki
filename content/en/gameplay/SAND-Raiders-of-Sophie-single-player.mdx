<Section icon={Map} title="Solo-Friendly Game Modes: Voyage vs Storm Dive">

For those engaging in **SAND Raiders of Sophie single player** sessions, choosing the right mode is the difference between a successful haul and a total loss of your Trampler. The game offers two distinct ways to explore the planet Sophie, each with its own risk profile for the solo survivor.

Voyage Mode is essentially the "persistent" experience. It allows you to enter the world, scavenge at your own pace, and extract when you feel the risk has become too high. There is no shrinking circle forcing you into a central meat-grinder, making it the superior choice for solo players who prefer a methodical, stealthy approach to looting.

| Mode Detail | Voyage (Solo Recommended) | Storm Dive (High Risk) |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Primary Objective** | Persistent Scavenging | High-Tier Loot/Survival |
| **Time Pressure** | None (Player-driven) | High (Closing Sandstorm) |
| **Player Density** | Low/Spread Out | High (Forced Proximity) |
| **Loot Quality** | Standard to High | Elite/Artifact Focus |
| **Best For** | Learning/Steady Progress | PvP Combat/Fast Rewards |

<Callout type="warning" title="Storm Dive Warning">
Solo players in Storm Dive are at a massive disadvantage during the final stages. Without a crew to man turrets while you steer, you are highly vulnerable to being boarded or outmaneuvered in the center of the storm.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Settings} title="Best Trampler Builds for Solo Raiders">

In **SAND Raiders of Sophie single player**, your Trampler is not just a vehicle; it is your mobile fortress, storage unit, and life support system. Large crews can build massive, multi-story "Ironclads" because they have the manpower to defend every deck. A solo player needs a "Ghost" build—something small, fast, and difficult to spot on the horizon.

When building your solo walker in the Trampler Editor, prioritize these specific compartments to maximize your survival chances:

| Module Type | Priority | Reason for Solo Players |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **High-Tier Engine** | ★★★★★ | Speed is your primary defense. You cannot outgun a crew, but you can outrun them. |
| **Small Storage** | ★★★★☆ | Large storage modules increase your profile. Take only what you need to survive. |
| **Repair Station** | ★★★★★ | You have no crew to fix legs during a fight. Automated or accessible repair is vital. |
| **Steering Deck** | ★★★★★ | Place this in a protected but visible area. Losing control of the walker is a death sentence. |
| **Armor Plates** | ★★★☆☆ | Heavy armor slows you down. Use light, reinforced plates on critical modules only. |

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Progression and Character Maintenance">

Even in **SAND Raiders of Sophie single player**, your progress is persistent. Your character's tech tree and inventory are tied to your selected region, but your Trampler blueprints are shared across all your characters. This allows you to experiment with different builds without losing your favorite designs.

If you find yourself in a situation where you have lost all your resources and your Trampler, the game provides a "recovery" path. You can join another team as a temporary crew member to earn resources, or start a new character to reset your basic supplies. However, for the true solo experience, maintaining a "rainy day" fund of resources at the orbital station is the best way to ensure you are never truly stranded.

| Resource Type | Use Case | Solo Retention Priority |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Scrap Metal** | Basic Repairs | High (Constant need) |
| **Fuel Cells** | Movement | Essential (Never leave without 2+ spares) |
| **Tech Components** | Upgrading Modules | Medium (Save for the station) |
| **Artifacts** | High-end Trading | Top (Primary goal of every run) |
